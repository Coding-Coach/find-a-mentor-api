Hey Mentors!

 

It's been a while, but we're excited to reach out to you today. We want to thank you all for being part of CodingCoach and helping the members of our community take that next step on the journey to becoming a software developer or advancing their career.

 

We're always thinking of ways to improve the experience for both mentors and mentees. For example, it's sometimes difficult to remember who's asked us to be their mentor. Every mentee introduces themselves in different way across a variety of channels since there isn't a formal way to make this connection on the CodingCoach platform. Did they ask me on Slack? Twitter? Email? I can't remember.

 

Soon we'll be launching new functionality that allows mentors and mentees to establish a connection using the codingCoach platform directly. Prospective mentees will now submit a mentorship request to you using in-app tools. You'll receive an email as well as seeing a new request after you log in. Once you review the request – and either accept or decline the request (be nice) – the mentee will be informed of your decision and the request will be closed.

 

Once you approve, we'll send an email to the mentee and only then, they will see your channels. If you decline their request, they won't see your contact information. With this change, both sides of the mentorship request will have a better understanding of what's happening, which will lead to a better outcome for everyone.

 

We really appreciate every mentor in CodingCoach! We want to remind you that if you're no longer available to take mentees, please set yourself as inactive.

 

If something is not working as expected or you have great ideas about this process or others, please let us know or create an issue or discussion in our GitHub organization.
